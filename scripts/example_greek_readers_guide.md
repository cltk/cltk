# Reader's Guide

**Pronunciation mode:** attic_5c_bce

## Sentence 1

> ὅτι μὲν ὑμεῖς , ὦ ἄνδρες Ἀθηναῖοι , πεπόνθατε ὑπὸ τῶν ἐμῶν κατηγόρων , οὐκ οἶδα :

> Translation (en-US): That you, O men of Athens, have suffered at the hands of my accusers, I do not know.

> Notes: The vocative formula ὦ ἄνδρες Ἀθηναῖοι is rendered as "O men of Athens." The negation chain οὐκ οἶδα is treated idiomatically as "I do not know." The perfect πεπόνθατε is taken as a present-state perfect: "have suffered."
